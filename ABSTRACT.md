## Abstract

The three-dimensional marine environment in which sharks, rays, and chimaeras live pose a unique challenge for spatial analyses. Most conventional Geographic Information Systems (GIS) workflows represent space as a two-dimensional plane. Commonly used species ranges datasets, like AquaMaps and IUCN Red List,  This abstraction of space works for terrestrial species, but fails to accurately represent the range of depths and vertical overlap of various marine habitat types. Additionally, fisheries operate at different depths depending on factors, like gear type. These reasons demonstrate the need for a reproducible and easy-to-use method for querying 3D space for shark species ranges and distributions. In this presentation, we share development on the marinehabitat3D open-source R package. The marinehabitat3D package provides a suite of tools and example workflows to query and utilize large marine datasets for characterising shark, ray, and chimaera habitat. The documented and transparent use of big data for shark, ray, and chimaera research can accelerate developing our understanding across the group.
